# Exemption (b)(5) — Deliberative-process privilege (note)

Exemption (b)(5) protects inter- and intra-agency communications that would be
privileged in civil litigation. Its most-invoked component is the
**deliberative-process privilege**, which shields material that is both:

1. **pre-decisional** — generated before the agency reaches a decision; and
2. **deliberative** — reflecting the give-and-take of the consultative process
   (recommendations, drafts, opinions), as opposed to purely factual material.

Purely factual content is generally **not** protected and should be released
unless it is so inextricably intertwined with deliberative material that it
cannot be reasonably segregated. Under the FOIA Improvement Act's 25-year
sunset, the deliberative-process privilege does not apply to records created 25
or more years before the request.
