# 5 USC 552(b) — The nine FOIA exemptions

The FOIA does not apply to matters that are:

- **(b)(1)** — specifically authorized under criteria established by an
  Executive order to be kept secret in the interest of national defense or
  foreign policy, and are in fact properly classified.
- **(b)(2)** — related solely to the internal personnel rules and practices of
  an agency.
- **(b)(3)** — specifically exempted from disclosure by another statute.
- **(b)(4)** — trade secrets and commercial or financial information obtained
  from a person and privileged or confidential.
- **(b)(5)** — inter-agency or intra-agency memoranda or letters that would not
  be available by law to a party in litigation with the agency (covers the
  deliberative-process, attorney-client, and attorney work-product privileges).
- **(b)(6)** — personnel and medical files and similar files the disclosure of
  which would constitute a clearly unwarranted invasion of personal privacy.
- **(b)(7)** — records or information compiled for law-enforcement purposes,
  to the extent disclosure would cause one of the enumerated harms.
- **(b)(8)** — information related to the examination, operation, or condition
  of financial institutions.
- **(b)(9)** — geological and geophysical information and data, including maps,
  concerning wells.

Under the FOIA Improvement Act of 2016, an agency may withhold only if it
reasonably foresees that disclosure would harm an interest protected by an
exemption, or disclosure is prohibited by law (the **foreseeable-harm**
standard).
