# Exemption (b)(6) — Personal-privacy balancing (note)

Exemption (b)(6) covers personnel, medical, and "similar files" the disclosure
of which would constitute a **clearly unwarranted invasion of personal
privacy**. Application requires a **balancing test**:

- weigh the **individual's privacy interest** in the information against
- the **public interest** in disclosure — measured by the extent to which
  disclosure would shed light on an agency's performance of its statutory
  duties (the "core purpose" of the FOIA).

"Similar files" is read broadly to cover any information that applies to a
particular individual. Where the privacy interest outweighs the public interest,
identifying details (names, contact information, signatures) are redacted while
the remainder of the record is released after reasonable segregation.
